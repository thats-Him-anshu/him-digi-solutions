How to Run Google Ads for Your Local Business in India (2026 Guide)

Himanshu, HIM Digi Solutions April 2026 8 min read

If you run a local business in India — a restaurant, a boutique, a coaching centre, a salon — Google Ads is one of the fastest ways to get customers who are already looking for what you offer. Unlike social media, Google targets people with active intent. They're searching for you. You just need to show up.

This guide covers exactly how to set up and run Google Ads as a local business owner in India in 2026 — without wasting money.

1. Understand What Google Search Ads Actually Do

When someone in Hosur or Bangalore types "best restaurant near me" or "digital marketing agency Hosur" — Google shows ads at the top of the results. You pay only when someone clicks. This is called Pay-Per-Click (PPC) advertising.

The key advantage: the person is already searching. They have intent. Converting them is much easier than interrupting someone on Instagram who wasn't thinking about you.

2. Choose the Right Keywords

Your keywords are the search terms you want to trigger your ad. For a local business, always target:

  • Location + service keywords: "digital marketing agency Hosur", "restaurant Hosur", "CA near me Krishnagiri"
  • Problem keywords: "how to get more customers for my business", "increase Instagram followers India"
  • Intent keywords: "hire digital marketer Tamil Nadu", "book table restaurant Hosur"

Use Google's free Keyword Planner tool (inside Google Ads) to find search volumes and costs per click in your area.

Pro tip: Start with 10–15 tightly focused keywords, not 100. More keywords = more wasted spend. Quality over quantity always wins in Google Ads.

3. Set a Realistic Budget

For a local business in a city like Hosur or Krishnagiri, ₹300–500/day (₹9,000–15,000/month) is a reasonable starting budget. In metro cities like Bangalore or Chennai, competition is higher and you may need ₹500–1,000/day.

Remember: you're paying per click, not per impression. A well-optimised campaign in Tier 2 Indian cities often delivers clicks for ₹15–40, making it very cost-effective.

4. Write Ads That Actually Get Clicked

Your ad has a headline (up to 30 characters × 3 headlines) and a description (90 characters × 2). Here's the formula that works:

  • Headline 1: Include your keyword — "Digital Marketing Hosur"
  • Headline 2: Your offer or USP — "Free Audit | 24/7 Support"
  • Headline 3: Social proof — "Trusted by Local Businesses"
  • Description: Expand on the benefit + clear call to action

5. Send Traffic to the Right Page

This is where most small businesses waste money. They send ad traffic to their homepage. Instead, create a dedicated landing page that:

  • Matches exactly what the ad promised
  • Has one clear call to action (call, fill form, or WhatsApp)
  • Loads fast on mobile (most Indian users are on mobile)
  • Has social proof (reviews, testimonials, results)

6. Track Conversions — Not Just Clicks

The biggest mistake: spending on ads without knowing which keywords and ads are generating actual leads. Set up Google Ads conversion tracking — it's free and takes 30 minutes. Track WhatsApp clicks, form submissions, and phone calls as conversions.

7. Optimise Weekly

Google Ads is not a "set and forget" tool. Check your campaigns weekly:

  • Pause keywords with high spend and zero conversions
  • Add negative keywords (searches you don't want to show for)
  • Test new ad copy every month
  • Increase budget on campaigns with strong ROAS
